KarlBenz is based on the blockchain technology data market and provides blockchain infrastructure for car users, manufacturers and service providers to share and exchange car data. Not only does it create a platform for collecting data on unwanted cars, but also it compensates for all the data generated by all car users, and useless car data becomes a source of revenue for different services. A new business concept and model was born. With the ecological economy that creates value in a virtuous circle, the KarlBenz blockchain is expected to be a leader in automotive data.

The KarlBenz blockchain is designed to process large amounts of time series data quickly and efficiently, combining the Practical Byzantine Fault Tolerant Algorithm (PBFT) and the Delegated Proof of Stake (DPoS) concept whick is similar to Tendermint's algorithm. For the first category, this approach can process more than 1000 transactions per second, eliminating the bandwidth waste that can occur when using Proof of Work (PoW) or Proof of Stake (PoS) coherency algorithms. Vehicles and internet of things device will generate large amounts of data and record this data in the blockchain, and KarlBenz will give the most computing resources and shared nodes (KarlBenz pass) Blocks create permissions instead of letting nodes compete for blocks. Therefore, the KarlBenz blockchain has the advantage of maintaining more than 1000 transactions per second (guaranteed 1000 TPS) by the certifier to save the block creation rights. Block creation can only be performed by the verifier in a majority of 2/3 of the protocol, so there is no branch problem to compensate the node participants (such as IoT devices or vehicles) with limited computing resources through the existing ownership authorization mechanism. In the consensus algorithm, candidates are selected as verifiers through the voting process of the network participants, informing their communities of their commitments and calculation specifications. However, there is a certifier selection issue when relying on the voting process.
